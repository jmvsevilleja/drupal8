What is Bad Credit?

In general, bad credit represents a list of previous payment defaults on your credit contracts, leading to the lack of ability to get accepted for another new credit. Below is a clear picture of where bad credit stands in general credit scoring (scoring may vary from every credit reporting agency):
  • Very good to Excellent credit score - 700 to 850
  • Good credit score - 680 to 699
  • Average or OK credit score - 620 to 679
  • Low credit score - 580 to 619
  • Poor credit score - 500 to 579
  • Bad credit score - 300 to 499
Bad credit is generally having a credit score at 300 to 499. You may have a bad credit many reasons. Reasons may include but not limited to late payments, payment defaults, identity theft, not enough credit history, bankruptcies, state and federal liens, and divorce resulting in bad debts. Credit bureaus or credit reporting agencies gather your credit history and put it together into a credit report which is accessible by you or any lending institutions upon request. Every single agency keeps its very own individual report, and your credit rating can differ from the other, because of errors or overlooked details. Despite the fact that you will see the records and background for every actual credit accounts on your credit history, your credit score will not be visible on your credit report.
